Civil Engineering and Beyond

Like every Civil engineer I wanted to make the world a better place by building roads, buildings and dams for communities.  However things took a turn in 2018 (my 3rd year) when a lady from one of the top engineering consulting companies came and presented on  how they used AI and virtual reality for their engineering projects. My inquisitiveness for tech was therefore born. In my final year studies I wanted to do research on something unique and disruptive, way beyond just 48 ways to make concrete. I therefore chose to do my final year dissertation on “The Impacts of the 4th Industrial Revolution on the Civil Engineering Industry” in that year I was able to network and meet other like-minded people in industry who were  well versed on that end. What excited me the most about my studies were the potential value propositions emerging tech had in place for the engineering industry.  A year later I was fortunate enough to work for the same company that had captured my attention in my 3rd year. I came in with this 4IR, innovative mindset ,where  at the time 4IR was somewhat of an annoying  buzzword in the building.  That year I decided that I needed to also ride the industry disruptive wave and upskill and future proof myself beyond my mere engineering capabilities. And it only made sense that I learn how to code. I therefore enrolled in an online data science program, where I learnt all about data science pipeline and also got exposed to machine learning. However, I didn't just want to become a data scientist, I wanted to enact both my engineering and tech background to help develop disruptive , innovative solutions.  2 years into my career, the reality is that not much of the  picture I had envisioned of innovation was happening in the industry - as yet. Although we had used some sophisticated engineering software to design built environment infrastructure there were some grey areas and room for more efficient solutions both on site and the office and I observed that there was a market for it especially in Africa. Last year I had the opportunity to present on an emerging technology  in the roads and transportation industry of my choice. I presented on the value propositions of adopting the digital twin model in transportation systems.  I further realized and concluded that it wasn't the engineering of the road that I was most interested in , rather the development of tech solutions and systems to make the road and it’s systems more efficient that intrigued me the most. .
